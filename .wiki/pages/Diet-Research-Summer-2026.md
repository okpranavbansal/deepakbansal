Successful management of fasting levels around **300 mg/dL** involves a balanced partnership between clinical medicine, Ayurvedic principles, and lifestyle consistency.

### Key Clinical Considerations:
- **Medication Synergy**: The current regimen (Daparyl M, Huminsulin, Panlipase) is a robust medical framework.

## Ayurvedic Evaluation (Summer Context)

The plan focuses on "Seasonal Optimization" (*Ritucharya*) to keep the body cool and the metabolism (*Agni*) steady during the summer months.

### 1. The Power of Chaach (Buttermilk)
- **Insight**: Unlike heavy curd (Dahi), Chaach is light, cooling, and aids digestion without blocking channels. It is the preferred summer probiotic in Ayurveda.
- **Preparation**: Best taken with roasted Jeera (Cumin) and a pinch of black salt.

### 2. Summer Grain Selection
- **Jau (Barley)**: Highly cooling and fiber-rich. It is excellent for balancing sugar levels while keeping the body temperate.
- **Jowar (Sorghum)**: A naturally cooling grain that is light on the stomach.
- **Multigrain Mix**: The updated mix prioritizes Barley and Jowar over heating grains like Bajra for the peak summer weeks.

### 3. Cooling Herbs & Teas
- **Chamomile**: Naturally cooling and calming. It helps reduce stress, which can indirectly help in managing sugar spikes.
- **Tulsi**: Supports metabolism and has a neutral-to-cooling effect when taken as a light tea.
- **Methi & Jeera**: Soaked Methi water remains a cornerstone for morning sugar stability.
