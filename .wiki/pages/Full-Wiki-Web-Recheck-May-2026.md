Patient context remains: Deepak Bansal, 53-year-old male, type 2 diabetes with HbA1c 12.7% on 2 May 2026, fasting glucose improved from 275.7 mg/dL to 184 mg/dL by 9 May 2026, LDL 181.7, triglycerides 238, Grade-2 fatty liver with 19 cm liver span, mild Grade-1 prostatomegaly, Daparyl M (dapagliflozin + metformin), Huminsulin, Panlipase 10000, pancreatitis/EPI concern, and ongoing chain smoking.

## Current High-Signal Conclusions

1. **Smoking remains the highest-leverage modifiable risk.** CDC guidance confirms that smoking worsens diabetes control and complications; quitting can make glucose easier to manage, but nicotine replacement should be clinician-supervised in diabetes because nicotine can raise glucose.
2. **SGLT2 sick-day safety stays non-negotiable.** Daparyl M contains dapagliflozin. FDA safety material continues to support urgent ketone/doctor evaluation for abdominal pain with nausea/vomiting, unusual fatigue, breathlessness, dehydration, fever, or poor intake, even if glucose is not very high.
3. **Fatty liver should be handled as MASLD risk stratification, not just "fat on USG."** AASLD/EASL-style workflows support FIB-4 first using age, AST, ALT, and platelets; if FIB-4 is indeterminate/high, FibroScan/VCTE, ELF, MRE, or hepatology review becomes more important.
4. **The ultrasound lowered risk but did not absolutely exclude every acute abdominal diagnosis.** The original report itself says poor acoustic window from obesity, bowel gas, and body habitus and asks for clinical/alternative imaging correlation. ACR/RadiologyInfo notes that CT or MRI/MRCP can be appropriate after a negative/questionable ultrasound if symptoms persist.
5. **Pancreatitis/EPI nutrition guidance supports small, frequent, low-fat meals plus PERT with food.** NIDDK supports small frequent meals, PERT when eating, alcohol avoidance, smoking cessation, fluid intake, and caffeine limitation in pancreatitis contexts.
6. **Supplements remain "doctor-review only," especially with liver disease and diabetes medicines.** NCCIH says diabetes supplement evidence is weak and supplement-medication interactions matter. LiverTox/NCCIH report rare but real liver injury signals for ashwagandha and bioavailable turmeric/curcumin products.
7. **Mild BPH/prostatomegaly is symptom-led.** NIDDK supports watchful waiting for slightly enlarged prostate when symptoms do not affect quality of life, with attention to caffeine/alcohol, evening fluids, activity, complete emptying, and urinary symptoms.

## Corrections Applied To The Wiki

### Imaging Language

Older wiki pages used "definitively ruled out" for gallbladder disease, kidney stone, and appendicitis after USG. That is now softened:

- Normal gallbladder/kidney/RIF findings make those diagnoses **less likely on that scan**.
- Because the sonologist documented a poor acoustic window and recommended clinical/alternative imaging correlation, persistent or worsening symptoms still require clinician review and possibly CT/MRI/MRCP.
- Gas/hepatic-flexure spasm and hepatomegaly-related discomfort remain plausible explanations for the 7 May episode, but they should not be used to ignore new severe pain.

### Diet And Herb Language

Older "protective shield" wording is kept only as historical context. The current patient-facing rule is:

- Use haldi, kali mirch, adrak, lahsun, vegetables, dals, grains, fruit, nuts, and seeds as measured food-level supports.
- Do not use giloy, kutki, ashwagandha, high-dose cinnamon, karela juice/extract, turmeric/curcumin capsules, green-tea extract, or multi-herb prostate/liver formulas without clinician approval and glucose/LFT monitoring.
- Avoid calling foods, spices, or herbs "cures" or "anti-cancer protocols." The real cancer-risk intervention is tobacco cessation plus ordinary evidence-based screening and care.

### Test Priority

The strongest "do soon" baseline set remains:

1. UACR / urine microalbumin-creatinine ratio plus urine routine.
2. CMP/LFT with creatinine/eGFR and electrolytes.
3. CBC with platelets.
4. FIB-4 calculation from age, AST, ALT, and platelets.
5. Dilated retinal/diabetic eye exam and foot/neuropathy exam.
6. Fecal elastase when digestive symptoms or Panlipase dose uncertainty are clinically active.
7. ECG/troponin urgently only for cardiac symptoms; ECG as baseline may still be reasonable because diabetes + LDL 181.7 + smoking + BP history creates high cardiovascular risk.
