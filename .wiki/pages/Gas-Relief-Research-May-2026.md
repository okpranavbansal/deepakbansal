## ⚠️ Why Gas Is Happening — Root Cause Analysis

Your father's gas is almost certainly caused by **a combination** of these factors:

### 1. Metformin Side Effect (Most Likely Contributor)
- Metformin is **well-known** to cause gas, bloating, and diarrhea — this is the #1 side effect reported across all sources (Cleveland Clinic, Healthline, Mayo Clinic)
- It alters gut bacteria and increases fermentation in the intestine
- If the dose was recently started or increased, this is very likely the primary cause

### 2. Panlipase 10000 Dosage May Be Too Low
- Panlipase 10000 means 10,000 lipase units per capsule
- Standard adult dosing for EPI starts at **40,000–50,000 lipase units per meal**
- If he is only taking **1 capsule (10,000 units)** per meal, **he is significantly underdosed**
- Underdosing causes: gas, bloating, fatty stools, abdominal discomfort
- **This needs urgent discussion with his doctor**

### 3. High-Fiber / Gas-Producing Diet
The current diet (which is medically excellent) includes many gas-producing foods:
- Multigrain atta (barley, jowar, ragi, methi dana, alsi)

## ⚠️ CRITICAL: The Sugar Warning

For someone with an HbA1c of 12.7%, every gram of hidden sugar matters.

1. **Verify "Sugar-Free" Label**: When buying Digene, Gelusil, or any antacid in Ambala, explicitly ask the pharmacist for the **Sugar-Free (SF)** version.
2. **Avoid "Fruit" Syrups**: Many generic digestive syrups (like Zymex, Aristozyme) contain heavy sugar syrups. Avoid these unless they are clearly marked sugar-free.
3. **Tablets are Safer**: Generally, chewable sugar-free tablets are even safer than gels as they contain fewer thickening agents that might impact sugar.
