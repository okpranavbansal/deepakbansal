# Medication Research: May 2026 Evaluation

## Overview of Clinical Regimen
The current treatment plan for Deepak Bansal involves a sophisticated, multi-pronged approach to manage severe hyperglycemia (fasting levels ~300 mg/dL) and potential digestive insufficiency.

### 1. Huminsulin (Insulin)
- **Mechanism**: Direct blood glucose lowering.
- **Role**: Essential for breaking "glucose toxicity" in patients with extremely high fasting levels. It provides immediate stabilization while long-term medications take effect.
- **Safety**: Requires consistent meal timing to prevent hypoglycemia.

### 2. Daparyl M (Dapagliflozin + Metformin)
- **Mechanism**:
    - **Dapagliflozin (SGLT2i)**: Promotes glucose excretion through the kidneys.
    - **Metformin**: Reduces hepatic glucose production and improves insulin sensitivity.
- **Role**: Long-term metabolic control and cardiovascular protection.
- **Requirement**: **Exceptional hydration** is mandatory because the SGLT2 inhibitor works through urine excretion.
- **14 May 2026 safety re-check**: Because dapagliflozin is an SGLT2 inhibitor, abdominal pain with nausea/vomiting, unusual fatigue, breathing difficulty, fever, dehydration, poor intake, or acute illness should trigger urgent doctor/ketone evaluation. Ketoacidosis can rarely occur even when glucose is not extremely high, so "sugar is okay" is not enough reassurance during a sick-day pattern.

### 3. Panlipase 10000 (Pancrelipase)
- **Mechanism**: Replaces digestive enzymes (Lipase, Protease, Amylase).
- **Role**: Treats Exocrine Pancreatic Insufficiency (EPI). EPI is often a secondary complication in chronic diabetic patients or those with pancreatic stress.
- **Best Practice**: Must be taken with the **first bite** of every meal to ensure proper nutrient absorption and prevent malnutrition.
- **14 May 2026 nutrition re-check**: NIDDK EPI guidance supports taking PERT/Panlipase when eating, using small frequent meals when needed, avoiding alcohol, and quitting smoking. Dose adequacy should be reviewed with the treating doctor if oily/foul stools, chronic diarrhea, weight loss, or persistent bloating continue.

## Emergency Medications
The following medications are kept at home for emergency use, specifically for suspected cardiac events:

### 1. Disprin (325mg)
- **Role**: Aspirin.
- **Action**: To chew immediately.

### 2. Sorbitrate (5mg)
- **Role**: Isosorbide Dinitrate.
- **Action**: To place under the tongue if there is severe chest pain (lowers heart workload).

### 3. Emergency Stabilization Kit
- **Atorvastatin (80mg) & Clopidogrel (300mg)**: High doses taken at once to stabilize a clot while in transit to the hospital.
