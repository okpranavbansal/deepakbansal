## 🔑 Key Findings — Organ by Organ

### 🫁 Liver — ATTENTION
- **Span**: 19 cm (enlarged; normal 13–16 cm)
- **Echogenicity**: Increased — suggestive of fatty infiltration
- **IHBR**: Not dilated
- **Focal lesions**: None
- **Grade**: Fatty Liver Grade-2 (moderate steatosis)

**Clinical significance**: Grade-2 fatty liver means 33–66% of hepatocytes contain fat droplets. The liver is visibly swollen (hepatomegaly at 19 cm). This is the **metabolic consequence** of:
- Uncontrolled diabetes (HbA1c 12.7% → insulin resistance → hepatic fat deposition)
- Dyslipidemia (Triglycerides 238, LDL 181.7 → excess lipid delivery to liver)
- Obesity (noted in the scan itself: "poor acoustic window due to obesity")

**Prognosis**: Grade-2 is still **fully reversible** with:
1. Blood sugar control (target HbA1c < 7%)
2. Weight loss of 5–10% body weight
3. Mediterranean-style diet (already partially in place)
4. Regular exercise (150 min/week moderate intensity)
5. Avoiding alcohol completely

**Risk if untreated**: Grade-2 can progress to:
- NASH (Non-Alcoholic Steatohepatitis) — inflammation + scarring
- Fibrosis → Cirrhosis → Liver failure (takes years, but prevention is critical)

- **Exercise**: Aim for 150+ minutes/week of moderate aerobic activity
- **Medications**: Discuss with doctor whether Pioglitazone or Vitamin E supplementation would help (evidence-based for NAFLD in diabetics)
