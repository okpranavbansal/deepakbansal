🆕Section 7 — Newly Reported Symptoms

🩺 High Blood Pressure (Hypertension)

Hypertension in a diabetic patient is extremely serious. Research shows 35–75% of cardiovascular risk in diabetics is attributable to hypertension. The 2025 AHA/ACC guidelines set a strict target of <130/80 mmHg for diabetic patients. His current combination of uncontrolled diabetes + hypertension + dyslipidemia creates the highest possible cardiovascular risk profile. ACE inhibitors or ARBs are the first-line medicines for diabetic hypertension — they also protect the kidneys.

🦠 Fungal Infection (Tinea Cruris) — Inner Thighs

This is NOT a random infection. The American Diabetes Association states directly: fungal infections in the groin and thighs are a classic complication of uncontrolled diabetes. High blood sugar creates a glucose-rich environment in skin folds where fungus thrives. Up to 70% of diabetics develop skin infections. With HbA1c at 12.7%, the skin is essentially feeding the fungus. Treatment requires antifungal cream + blood sugar control — the infection will keep recurring without controlling diabetes.

🔗 The Connection: Both hypertension and fungal infection are direct consequences of uncontrolled diabetes. The fungal infection is a visible, external alarm signal that the body is struggling with high blood sugar internally. The hypertension compounds the kidney and heart risk from diabetes dramatically.

🦠Fungal Infection — Care Protocol
1
See a Dermatologist

Get prescription antifungal cream (Clotrimazole or Luliconazole). Apply twice daily for minimum 4 weeks. Do NOT stop early even if it improves.

2
Keep Area Dry

After bathing, dry inner thighs completely with a clean towel. Pat, don't rub. Moisture feeds fungus.

3
Loose Cotton Clothing

Wear loose cotton underwear and trousers. Avoid synthetic/tight clothing that traps moisture and heat in the groin area.

4
Antifungal Powder

After drying, apply antifungal powder (Clotrimazole powder) to inner thighs to prevent moisture buildup — especially in summer.

5
Control Blood Sugar First

The infection will KEEP COMING BACK without sugar control. The ADA confirms: fungal infections in diabetics are directly driven by high glucose in the skin. Treat both simultaneously.

6
Do Not Share Towels

Tinea Cruris is contagious. Use a personal towel. Wash with hot water. Change underwear daily. Avoid scratching — spreads the infection.
